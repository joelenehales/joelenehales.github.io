Experience

I've spent the past 2.5+ years developing software for research in various fields of astrophysics, ranging from interstellar molecules to galaxies.


Astrochemistry

Sept. 2022 - Aug. 2023

In my most recent research project, I analyzed how interstellar carbon molecules vary across star-forming regions with different physical properties! I specifically looked at dust emission features in the Tarantula Nebula, a star-forming region in a nearby galaxy with a different chemical composition than ours, and compared the to those we see in star-forming regions in our own galaxy. This involved developing code in Python to process raw data from the James Webb Space Telescope, extract one-dimensional spectra, and measure the intensities and profiles of dust emission features.


Galactic Astronomy

May 2022 - Aug. 2022

After the third year of my undergraduate degree, I spent a summer categorizing the objects in nearby galaxies. I developed a Python code to automate the validation of object classifications between literature-based astrophysical databases using satellite data. I contributed to the the open-source code for Galaxy Data Mines, a command-line tool to crossmatch sources and compare object classifications between astronomical databases. I updated match algorithms to include newly added object classifications, and implemented support for a wider variety of result output formats.

Exoplanet Astronomy

May 2021 - May 2022

In my first research project, I spent a year searching for exoplanets around ultra-cool stars, some of the most likely candidates to host habitable planets! In this project, I developed a pipeline in Python to extract light curves from satellite data of stars, measure stellar rotation periods using periodogram and Gaussian process regression methods, and evaluate the significance of the results. I used SSH to connect to a remote computer network and run the pipeline on a dataset of 3,726 stars. This allowed me to identify a selection nearby stars with a high probability of detecting habitable-zone Earth-like exoplanets. A portion of these stars are expected to be used as the target catalog for an upcoming satellite for exoplanet astrophysics!
